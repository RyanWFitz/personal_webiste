A little about me: I enjoy many things in life, but possibly what I enjoy the most is the journey that I am taken on while pursuing my hobbies.

Snow Skiing

For as long as I can remember, I have always snow skied. My family has been involved in the sport for over 50 years, and as such my father helped coach me from the moment I could walk. This sport allows me to express myself and show how much my dedication is able to pay off, and it opens up opportunities.

Whenever I bring up sports, snow skiing is my favorite activity there is and it has helped me develop as both a person and a professional. As a young kid, I always wanted to compete at the top level of competition, but a knee injury when I was an early teenager made it so I was unable to continue that path.

While I may no longer be trying to compete, I still look to continue my passion chasing the hardest trails and the hardest lines I can find. Typically, this leads me to many new places, whether its skiing the open slopes of Mammoth Mountain or finding the largest powder bank in Alta, Utah, I will always look for new experiences. I've had the pleasure of skiing a large majority of the ski areas in the United States and have a long term goal of trying to visit all of the resorts within North America, it's more of a lofty goal but I think it would be fun to achieve.

The other benefit snow skiing has brought me is the people that I have met along the way. I have had the opportunity to meet numerous new friends that I have conected with over our love of the sport. I was even lucky enough to participate in one of my closest friend's wedding in Powder Mountain, Utah, where we had the reception at the top of the mountain!

Water Skiing

Water skiing is a sport that has been around for many years, but is usually something that families do sparingly on that one trip they took to the lake 15 years ago. For a number of families, includiong my own, it is something that we travel the country to do, competing in tournaments in order to qualify for higher tier competitions.

With water skiing being as small as it is, it surprisingly has a very passionate backing in collegiate sports. I graduated with a BA from Arizona State University, where I spent time as the captain of the team at the school. This position had me take responsibility of both the men's and women's team roster, where I was held accountable for about 40 other student athletes. With 8-10 tournaments a year, including both a regional and national tournament, we remained very busy throughout the school year.

The relationships that I have been able to build over the years of competing have been impactful in many ways. A large reason why I moved back to California from Arizona was to be able to be around the people I grew up competing with, who are now some of my closest friends. To this day I still compete in tournaments at an amateur level but it is more out of my love for the sport than the need to satisfy a competitive impulse.

Volleyball

The newest of the sports that I have played is volleyball, and it was something that I never saw myself doing. I was introduced to the sport by a good friend of mine who wanted me to meet new people when I moved to San Diego. This has become the hobby that I do weekly, and I have quickly devloped a love for the game.

Much of this love has been for the friends that I have met. When I began playing, it was in a simple group of friends playing casually. Since then, we have created a large group of over 60+ members that actively play every Sunday morning.

Over the past several years I have progressively gotten more proficient at the sport to the points where I am now comfortable enought to play in higher level tournaments. I will even be moving on to attempt to achieve my minor league rating over the next few years. These people have created a comfortable home for me to return to every weekend, and partially helped push me to begin my path of learning to code.
